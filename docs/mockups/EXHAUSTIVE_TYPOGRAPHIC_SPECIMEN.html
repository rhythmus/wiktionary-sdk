γράφω (gráphō) v. trans. & intran.
Principal Parts: fut. γράψω, aor. ἔγραψα, perf. γέγραφα, perf. pass. γέγραμμαι, aor. pass. ἐγράφην.
Pronunciation: [ɡrá.pʰɔː] (Attic, c. 5th BC); [ˈɣra.fo] (Koine/Modern)

I. Etymology & Origins

Proto-Hellenic *grápʰō ← Proto-Indo-European *gerbʰ- (“to scratch”).
Cognates: Old English ceorfan (to carve), German kerben (to notch), Armenian керуам (to scrape).

II. Senses & Citations

  • 1. Graphical Representation. To scratch, draw, or delineate lines on a surface; to graze. Hom. Il. 17. 599 ἐπιλίγδην γράψεν it grazed [the shoulder] slightly. Hdt. 4. 88 γράψας ἐς πίνακα πᾶσαν τὴν γέфуран having painted the whole bridge on a tablet.
  • 2. Writing & Composition. To express by written characters; to record, register, or enroll. Thuc. 1. 1 Θουκυδίδης Ἀθηναῖος ξυνέγραψε τὸν πόλεμον Thucydides the Athenian wrote the history of the war. Law 2b. To propose a law (v. mid. γράφομαι).
  • 3. Judicial Action. Attic Law To bring a public indictment; to prosecute. γράφεσθαί τινα γραφήν to bring a public action against someone.

IV. Usage Notes

Originally referred to the act of scratching on bark or tablets (cf. Mycenaean ka-ra-wi). In Attic prose, distinguished from γράφειν νόμον (to write/draft) vs. γράφεσθαι νόμον (to move for adoption).
